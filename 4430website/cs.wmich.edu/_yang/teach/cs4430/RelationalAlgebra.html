CS4430 Assignment 1: Relational Algebra

Assume a book store has a mail-order database with the following schema of relations: (Primary keys are underlined. Meaning of each attribute is self-explanatory by its name.)

EMPLOYEES: <ENO, ENAME, ZIP, HIRE_DATE>;
BOOKS: <ISBN, BNAME, QUANTITY, PRICE>;
CUSTOMERS: <CNO, CNAME, STREET, ZIP, PHONE>;
ORDERS: <ONO, CNO, ENO, RECEIVED, SHIPPED>;
ORDER_DETAILS: <ONO, ISBN, QTY>;
ZIPCODES: <ZIP, CITY, STATE>;

Convert the following queries into relational algebraic expressions:

  1. list customers (names) the zip of whose address is either 49008 or 49009.
  2. list employees (names) who have customers in Indiana.
  3. list employees (names) who have both 49008-zipcode customers and 49009-zipcode customers.
  4. list customers (CNOs and names) who live in Kalamazoo, excluding a special customer who's CNO is 1111.
  5. list customers (names) who've ordered books through an employee named 'Jones'.
  6. list customers (names) who've NOT ordered the book "Database Systems".
  7. all possible pairs of books (BNAMEs). (Don't include 2 of the same books in a pair).
  8. all possible pairs of books (BNAMEs) where the first has a price of 24.99 and the second has a price of 19.99.
  9. customers (names) who ordered at least one book that customer #1111 ordered.
  10. customers (names) who ordered all the books as customer #11111 ordered (although, they may have ordered additional books).

Prepare your answers using a word processor and submit to the WMU e-learning portal.
